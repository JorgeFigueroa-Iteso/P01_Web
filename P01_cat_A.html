Category A: Albums and lore

Blurryface (2015)

Blurryface is more than just an album; it's a journey into the psyche of Tyler Joseph, one half of the duo that makes up Twenty One Pilots. Blurryface embodies Tyler's insecurities and fears, personified as a character that he must confront throughout the album. Each track delves into different aspects of this struggle, making the album a mosaic of human emotion and inner conflict. The album traverses through genres effortlessly, from the reggae vibes of "Ride" to the dark pop of "Stressed Out," each song adding a layer to the complex identity of Blurryface. This album is a battleground where fears, doubts, and ultimately, the pursuit of self-acceptance, play out in a symphony of emotional depth and musical innovation.

Trench (2018)

Trench invites fans into a sprawling narrative universe, rich with symbolism and a deep-seated story of escape and resistance. The album introduces us to a dystopian world governed by the oppressive regime of Dema, ruled by nine bishops, with the central antagonist being Nico (linked to Blurryface). The narrative follows the protagonist, Clancy, as he attempts to escape Dema with the help of the Banditos, a rebel group. Songs like "Jumpsuit" and "Nico and the Niners" are anthems of rebellion and hope, encapsulating the themes of fighting against one's own demons and societal constraints. Trench is not just an album; it's an immersive experience that extends into music videos, a dedicated website, and cryptic messages, inviting fans to decipher and engage with the lore of Twenty One Pilots.

Heathens (2016)

While Heathens is technically a single from the soundtrack of the film "Suicide Squad," it fits seamlessly into the world of Twenty One Pilots' music and storytelling. The song delves into themes of alienation and camaraderie among outcasts, echoing the sentiments of not judging a book by its cover or misunderstanding those who are different. "Heathens" serves as a bridge between the band's existing lore and the broader themes of acceptance and understanding in the face of prejudice. Its dark, haunting melody complements the introspective lyrics, making it a standout track that resonates with the band's exploration of mental health and societal issues.

Vessel (2013)

Vessel is the vessel for Twenty One Pilots' breakthrough, introducing a wider audience to their genre-defying sound and deeply personal lyricism. The album is a collection of re-recorded tracks from their earlier work and new songs that together form a cohesive narrative about growth, existential angst, and the search for meaning. Tracks like "Car Radio" and "Holding on to You" confront the listener with the quiet moments of introspection and the loud cries against despair. Vessel's lore is not woven through a fictional narrative like Trench but through the real-life experiences and emotional vulnerabilities that Tyler and Josh Dun (drummer) navigate, making it a relatable and powerful album that connects on a profoundly human level.
